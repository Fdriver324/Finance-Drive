FAQ

Common Questions

How do I buy FDRIVE?
+
Install MetaMask, add the Polygon network, get some MATIC for gas fees (about $1), then go to QuickSwap or Uniswap and swap USDT or MATIC for FDRIVE. The contract address will be published on launch day.
Can the 1,000,000 supply ever increase?
+
Absolutely not. The 1,000,000 cap is hardcoded in the FDriveToken smart contract on Polygon. The entire supply was minted once at deployment. There is no mint function for additional tokens — not even the owner can create more. Verifiable by anyone on PolygonScan.
How does the FDRIVE cashback work?
+
Cashback is always 1% of the ride value in USD. That dollar amount is divided by the current FDRIVE price to determine how many tokens you receive. As FDRIVE appreciates, fewer tokens are distributed per ride — protecting the fixed 1,000,000 supply from inflation.
How does the DeFi lending work?
+
Deposit FDRIVE as collateral in the FDriveLending smart contract. Borrow up to 95% of the collateral value in USDT. Interest accrues at a variable rate based on protocol utilization. Repay USDT + interest at any time to get your FDRIVE back. If LTV exceeds 97%, the contract auto-liquidates.
Why only 15% commission?
+
Because drivers deserve more. Uber and 99 charge 25-30%, leaving drivers with 70-75% of each fare. Finance Drive charges a maximum of 15%, giving drivers 85%. We generate revenue from DeFi protocol fees and ecosystem growth — not from squeezing drivers.
Which wallet should I use?
+
MetaMask is the most popular and easiest option. You can also use Trust Wallet, Coinbase Wallet, or any EVM-compatible wallet. Just make sure to add the Polygon network (chainId: 137) and you're good to go.
